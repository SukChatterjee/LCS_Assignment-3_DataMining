The Three-Part Data Problem
What we CAN say
631 CPT and 614 OPT placements confirmed across 191 and 137 employers. These are verified international-friendly employers — a reliable minimum baseline.
What we CANNOT say
We cannot rank or score employers by international friendliness. Missing CPT/OPT data does not mean no international hiring — it means the form was never required to capture it.
What LCS should DO
Add work authorization as a required field (dropdown) in the co-op approval form. Within 2 semesters, LCS will have reliable data to build a verified "intl-friendly employer" list.
Strategic Recommendations
Three actions LCS can take now to grow employer diversity, fix data quality, and improve student outcomes
Strategy 01 · Employer Growth
Re-engage At-Risk & New Employers Through Talent Competitions
76 at-risk employers (Meritor, Anthem, TJX, Givaudan, Emerson…) averaged 4+ years of active hiring before going silent in 2022–23. 502 new employers haven't moved beyond a first hire. Traditional outreach emails get ignored.
Suggested Action
Host quarterly hackathons and case study competitions co-sponsored by at-risk and high-potential employers. Companies receive real problem-solving outputs and direct exposure to student talent — a far more compelling value proposition than a job posting. Structure events by vertical (insurance analytics, supply chain, fintech) to match Lindner's major concentration.
Expected impact: Re-activating 20–30% of at-risk employers (15–22 companies) at historical averages adds 200–400 placements annually to the pipeline.
Process 02 · Data Infrastructure
Add Required Work Authorization Field to the Co-op Approval Form
97.5% of co-op records have no work authorization data. Page 4 of this dashboard cannot reliably identify international-friendly employers because the form never required this field. International students, their advisors, and employers all make decisions with incomplete information.
Suggested Action
Add server-side validation making Student Work Authorization a required dropdown field: US Citizen/Permanent Resident · F-1 CPT · F-1 OPT · H-1B Sponsorship · Other. Mirror the existing CPT approval flow — which already requires documentation — as the implementation model. No new system needed; this is a form-field addition with a required validator.
Expected impact: Fill rate rises from 2.5% → 95%+ within 2 semesters, enabling a verified "international-friendly employer" filter in the student-facing co-op portal.
Policy 03 · Compliance
Make Co-op Form Completion Mandatory — Modeled on the CPT Approval Precedent
56.3% of pre-2026 records are stuck in "Approved" — never closed as completed. Stale records degrade every KPI, distort wage benchmarking, and make employer health analysis unreliable. International CPT students must complete a formal approval form. Domestic students face no equivalent obligation — creating an asymmetric compliance gap.
Suggested Action
Require co-op form completion and status update as a graduation or credit requirement, mirroring the CPT/OPT approval precedent. Implement a two-stage flow: (1) offer confirmation within 2 weeks of start date, (2) completion confirmation within 2 weeks of end date. Automate reminder emails at both triggers.
Expected impact: Stale record rate → 0% for new cohorts; creates a complete, auditable co-op system supporting wage transparency and employer accountability reporting.
Implementation Strategies
Concrete outreach plans to move these recommendations from plan to action
Strategy A · Partnership Outreach
Invite Companies to Co-Host Talent Competitions with Lindner
LCS reaches out to at-risk and high-potential employers proposing joint hackathons and case study competitions. Students get a fair chance to demonstrate real skills in front of hiring managers — not just a resume in a stack. Companies receive live problem-solving outputs, talent pipeline access, and on-campus brand visibility at low marginal cost. Framed as a partnership, not a recruitment drive.
Approach
Contact companies by industry vertical (insurance analytics, supply chain, fintech, consulting). Propose a co-branded event format: LCS supplies students and logistics; employer supplies the real business problem, judging panel, and prize sponsorship.
Why it works
At-risk employers already know UC talent — they need a reason to re-engage that isn't another cold email. New employers get a no-commitment way to evaluate students before committing to a co-op slot. Both sides win without the friction of a traditional job requisition.
Suggested cadence
One event per semester per industry vertical · 3–4 events per year · Start with the top 30 priority employers from the worklist.
Expected lift: Direct exposure for 200+ students per event · 15–22 re-activated employers per academic year.
Strategy B · Alumni Network Activation
Leverage UC Alumni in Leadership Roles at Target Employers
LCS reviewed LinkedIn profiles of directors, VPs, and C-suite contacts at priority employers and found a significant number are UC alumni. These individuals are high-conversion outreach targets — they already have personal affinity for the university and understand the quality of Lindner talent firsthand.
Approach
Build a prioritized list of UC alumni in senior roles (Director, VP, C-suite) at at-risk and high-potential employers. Reach out through the UC Alumni Association channels or direct LinkedIn messaging. Ask them to champion co-op hiring internally and spread word-of-mouth among peer networks.
Why it works
Warm outreach beats cold outreach every time. Alumni are already predisposed to say yes — they just need a specific, low-effort ask. Internal advocacy from a decision-maker opens doors that LCS can't open from the outside.
Execution
Cross-reference the 162 high-priority employer list with the UC Alumni Association database. Send a personalized outreach template asking for a 20-minute conversation. Frame it as "reconnecting with UC" rather than "recruiting for Lindner".
Expected lift: 3–5x higher response rate than cold outreach · opens doors at employers that would otherwise ignore general LCS emails.
